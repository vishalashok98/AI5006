\section{Problem}
Find the co ordinates of the point when the line through
\myvec{3\\-4\\-5} and \myvec{2\\-3\\1} crosses the plane 
\begin{math}
\myvec{ 2\\-3\\1}^T\vec{x}=7   
\end{math}
and perpendicular to the two lines

	
	
\section{Explanation}
Equation of the plane is 
\newline
\begin{align}
\myvec{ 2&-3&1}\vec{x}=7\label{eq:1}    
\end{align}

\newline
Direction vector of line passing through points \myvec{3\\-4\\-5} and \myvec{2\\-3\\1} is given by 
\newline
$\vec{m}$=\myvec{3\\-4\\-5}-\myvec{2\\-3\\1}=\myvec{1\\-1\\-6} 
\newline
\newline
Equation of a line passing through the point $\vec{a}$ and having direction vector $\vec{m}$ is given by:
\begin{align}
 \vec{x} =\vec{a} + \lambda\vec{m}\label{eq:1}
\end{align}
where $\lambda$ is some constant.
Parametric equations of line passing through \myvec{3\\-4\\-5} and \myvec{2\\-3\\1} is
\newline
\begin{center}


\vec{x} &=\myvec{ 2\\ -3\\ 1} + $\lambda$\myvec{ 1 \\-1\\-6} \label{eq:1}
\end{center}
\section{Solution}
Since the line intersects plane, by substituting parametric equation of line in equation of plane we get
\begin{align}
\myvec{ 2&1&1}(\vec{a}+\lambda\vec{m})=7    
\end{align}



\newline
\begin{align}
\myvec{ 2&1&1}\vec{a}+\myvec{ 2&1&1}\lambda\vec{m}=7
\end{align}
\newline
\begin{align}
\myvec{ 2&1&1}\myvec{ 2\\ -3\\ 1}+\myvec{ 2&1&1}\myvec{ 1 \\-1\\-6}\lambda=7
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\newline
\lambda=-1
\end{align}
Substituting the value of $\lambda$ in parametric equations we get point of intersection as \myvec{1\\-2\\7}
